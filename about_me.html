Hi, I'm Milan!

I'm a college graduate with a Bachelor's in Computer Science.

I started having an interest in coding after trying out AP Computer Science A during my sophomore year of high school. I had an amazing teacher at the time, Mrs. Whitlock, who was very enthusiastic about teaching Java to her students. While I had struggled to get the hang of coding at first, I found that I actually liked the challenge and how satisfying it felt to finally solve the problems and errors in the code.

The summer after my sophomore year, I applied and interned at CareerBuilder, which had a Girls In Tech Program at the time that aimed to help more women enter the tech field. A fellow intern and I helped create a website for the Project Integrity team, learning introductory Ruby on Rails, HTML, and CSS along the way.

It was interesting to work and code for the summer, and I had met a lot of fun and helpful colleagues during that time, too! I had decided since then that I would pursue computer science as a career path. So far I've dabbled in other programming languages such as Python, Javascript, PHP, and C#.


While I do like to code, I also have a vested interest in art. I took up art as a hobby around eighth grade, doodling and creating original characters on loose pieces of paper. I actually used part of the money I earned from the CareerBuilder internship to buy myself a drawing tablet, and I've been a practicing digital artist ever since. In my spare time, I participate in fanzines and passion projects.
